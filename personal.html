Personal Interests

This page documents my personal interests.

Books

This post gives a rough idea of the kind of books I am interested in. Currently, I am hugely into the works of Becky Chambers (Wayfarers, To Be Taught If Fortunate), Max Gladstone (The Craft Sequence, Bookburners, This Is How You Lose The Time War), N.K. Jemisin (The Inheritance Trilogy, The Broken Earth Trilogy), and Brandon Sanderson (The Cosmere Sequence, including Mistborn, Warbreaker and The Stormlight Archive). About a year ago, I also fell in love with The Vorkosigan Saga by Lois McMaster Bujold.

While epic fantasy doorstoppers are my first love, I've recently started a foray into fantasy novellas and short novels, especially those published by Tor.com publishing.

I was also a major fan of the Star Wars Expanded Universe (now known as Star Wars: Legends). Grand Admiral Thrawn is my favourite Star Wars character, Timothy Zahn is my favourite Star Wars writer and Luke Skywalker+Mara Jade is my OTP. As far as I'm concerned, if Mara no longer exists in the newfangled Disney canon, then the newfangled Disney canon does not exist for me. (Okay, maybe that's not precisely true…)

I love comic books, though I now largely consume them through adaptations only. My favourite comic book run is Geoff Johns and Peter Tomasi's on Green Lantern; Green Lantern: Rebirth was one of my two gateways into superhero comics. The other gateway comic was Jeph Loeb's run on Superman/Batman. My favourite mini-series is probably Grant Morrison's All-Star Superman. In general, I'm a DC guy, but I really adored Greg Pak's run on The Incredible Hercules (a Marvel publication). If I get to see a cinematic adaptation of JLA/Avengers, I will die a man satisfied with his life.

In recent times (especially since the release of Avengers: Infinity War and Avengers: Endgame), I've been reconsidering my loyalty to DC.

My favourite book series of all time is The Wheel of Time, by Robert Jordan, though my favourite author is probably Isaac Asimov. The question of which is my favourite book is much more difficult to answer so I'm not going to attempt to do it; however, Good Omens: The Nice and Accurate Prophecies of Agnes Nutter, Witch by Terry Pratchett and Neil Gaiman is probably up there somewhere on the list (you should watch the delightful adaptation of it on Amazon Prime!).

Games

Growing up, except for the short time I owned a PlayStation 2, the only videogames I used to play were real-time strategy games. My favourite videogame is probably Age of Mythology, and I adore the entire Age of Empires sequence. I also loved Warcraft III, and Blizzard's insistence towards no longer making Warcraft RTS games is my biggest gripe with the World of Warcraft MMORPG. I stopped playing videogames regularly around the same time I retired my PlayStation (that is, around 2008).

I love playing cards; in particular, I like rummy, 29 and hearts. In general, I prefer trick-taking games.

These days, I am really into modern boardgames. I enjoy some classics (particularly Cluedo, Stratego, Risk and Scrabble), but I mostly prefer modern Euro games, with a sprinkling of Ameritrash and a party game or two.

My favourite modern game is Dominion; I play online with the handle yahas. I've recently started playing Terra Mystica on snellman, again with the handle yahas. I also play a bunch of other games (particularly, Dice Forge, Kingdomino, Puerto Rico, Siam, 7 Wonders, Stone Age, Takenoko, Tokaido, Carcassone, 7 Wonders: Duel, Santorini, Love Letter, Jaipur and Through the Ages) on Board Game Arena, again, with the handle yahas. Offline, my favourite 2-player game is 7 Wonders: Duel, my favourite social deduction game is Avalon and my favourite party game is Codenames. It's much much harder to pin down my favourite heavy game, so I won't even try - but I like Scythe enough to actual own it, instead of just playing other people's copies. The list of games I physically own are on my BGG profile.

I play IRL with the Board Gamers of Delhi whenever I am in Delhi.

One of the best games I have played, which requires no resources but people to play, is Contact.

TV Shows and Movies

I don't watch a lot of movies, but when I do, they're almost exclusively either adaptations from other media, or they're rom-coms. I am a big fan of the Marvel Cinematic Universe, and the list of the movies I keep rewatching again includes 10 Things I Hate About You and Begin Again.

Two TV shows that had a huge impact on me as a kid were Chuck and Psych. Chuck is possibly single-handedly responsible for my early forays into alternate or indie rock. I was also very into cartoons (both western and anime) as a kid, some favourites include, the entire DC Animated Universe (particularly Justice League), Cardcaptor Sakura, Digimon and Fullmetal Alchemist. I really want to restart watching them (especially Young Justice), but I really haven't come around to it.

These days I don't have too much time to watch too many shows, but I really enjoyed the first few seasons of the Arrowverse and the Marvel Netflix TV shows.

One modern web television series that I absolutely adore is the The Lizzie Bennet Diaries. It's available for free online, and is a New Media adaptation of Pride and Prejudice.

Music

When I was younger, I largely listened only to rock and alternate rock. I have since expanded my tastes to country, progressive rock and some pop. 3 Rounds and a Sound by Blind Pilot is my favourite alt rock song, the Coke Studio (Pakistan) rendition of Tajdar-e-Haram by Atif Aslam is my favourite song in a Hindustani language, A.R. Rahman is my favourite Bollywood musician, Begin Again is my favourite original soundtrack from a motion picture and Hey Jude is my favourite song by the Beatles.

My current most listened to song is The Dream Synopsis (EP Version) by The Last Shadow Puppets.

History and Mythology

History and mythology are my first and second love (most likely because of the fact that they are the respective subjects of the games I played most in my childhood, namely Age of Empires and Age of Mythology). Crash Course is a good place to start learning about these fascinating subjects.
