Books

This post gives a rough idea of the kind of books I am interested in. Currently, I am hugely into the works of Jim Butcher (The Dresden Files, Codex Alera, The Cinder Spires), Max Gladstone (The Craft Sequence),and Brandon Sanderson (The Cosmere Sequence, including Mistborn, Warbreaker and the Stormlight Archive). Barring epic fantasy doorstoppers, I've recently started a foray into fantasy novellas, especially those published by Tor.com publishing.

I was also a major fan of the Star Wars Expanded Universe (now known as Star Wars: Legends). Grand Admiral Thrawn is my favourite Star Wars character, Timothy Zahn is my favourite Star Wars writer and Luke Skywalker+Mara Jade is my OTP. As far as I'm concerned, if Mara no longer exists in the newfangled Disney canon, then the newfangled Disney canon does not exist for me. (Okay, maybe that's not precisely true…)

I love comic books, though I now largely consume them through adaptations only. My favourite comic book run is Geoff Johns and Peter Tomasi's on Green Lantern; Green Lantern: Rebirth was one of my two gateways into superhero comics. The other gateway comic was Jeph Loeb's run on Superman/Batman. My favourite mini-series is probably Grant Morrison's All-Star Superman. In general, I'm a DC guy, but I really adored Greg Pak's run on The Incredible Hercules (a Marvel publication). If I get to see a cinematic adaptation of JLA/Avengers, I will die a man satisfied with his life.

My favourite book series of all time is The Wheel of Time, by Robert Jordan, though my favourite author is probably Isaac Asimov. The question of which is my favourite book is much more difficult to answer so I'm not going to attempt to do it; however, Good Omens: The Nice and Accurate Prophecies of Agnes Nutter, Witch by Terry Pratchett and Neil Gaiman is probably up there somewhere on the list.

The (largely complete) list of books I have read can be found on my Goodreads profile. It also has reviews of stories that I have read in the recent past.

Games

Growing up, except for the short time I owned a PlayStation 2, the only videogames I used to play were real-time strategy games. My favourite videogame is probably Age of Mythology, and I adore the entire Age of Empires sequence. I also loved Warcraft III, and Blizzard's insistence towards no longer making Warcraft RTS games is my biggest gripe with the World of Warcraft MMORPG. I stopped playing videogames regularly around the same time I retired my PlayStation (that is, around 2008).

I love playing cards; in particular, I like rummy, 29 and hearts. In general, I prefer trick-taking games, and I have recently started learning bridge.

These days, I am really into modern boardgames. I enjoy some classics (particularly Cluedo, Stratego, Risk and Scrabble), but I mostly prefer modern Eurogames, with a sprinkling of Ameritrash and a party game or two.

My favourite modern game is Dominion; I play online with the handle yahas. I've recently started playing Terra Mystica on snellman, again with the handle yahas. Offline, my favourite 2-player game is 7 Wonders: Duel, my favourite social deduction game is Avalon and my favourite party game is Codenames.

One of the better games I have played that require no resources is Contact.

Music

I have since expanded my tastes to country, progressive rock and some pop. 3 Rounds and a Sound by Blind Pilot is my favourite alt rock song, the Coke Studio (Pakistan) rendition of Tajdar-e-Haram by Atif Aslam is my favourite song in a Hindustani language, A.R. History and Mythology

History and mythology are my first and second love (most likely because of the fact that they are the respective subjects of the games I played most in my childhood, namely Age of Empires and Age of Mythology). Crash Course is a good place to start learning about these fascinating subjects.
