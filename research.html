Research Interests

My undergraduate projects and preprints give a fairly good idea of what kind of mathematics interests me.

Broadly speaking, I am interested in number theory and combinatorics. More specifically, I enjoy problems amenable to analytic techniques.

Within number theory, I lean towards the analytic, combinatorial and elementary aspects of number theory.

Within combinatorics, I have recently gotten interested in the application of the framework of algebraic ideas known as the polynomial method onto problems in discrete geometry.

I also have a tangential interest in theoretical computer science, particularly computational complexity.
