Research Interests

My projects give a fairly good idea of what kind of mathematics interests me.

Broadly speaking, I an interested in Number Theory. Part of what appeals to me is the sheer background one needs to do modern number theory. The interconnections with other fields often originally seem unrelated, although I'm not sure how unexpected people find it anymore; I certainly was very surprised the first time I learnt, say, that the distribution of prime numbers depended on the zeroes of the Riemann -function).

Within number theory, I lean towards the analytic, combinatorial and elementary aspects of number theory; I know very little about the algebraic aspects of number theory beyond the basics.

In particular, I am interested in additive number theory, multiplicative number theory (including the theory of the Riemann function), sieve theory and additive combinatorics. I am also interested tangentially in computational aspects of number theory, and the applications of additive combinatorics and number theory to theoretical computer science.
