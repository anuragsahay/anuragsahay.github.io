Research Interests

My projects give a fairly good idea of what kind of mathematics interests me.

Broadly speaking, I an interested in all forms of Number Theory. If you look at the historical underpinnings of the subject, almost all the great mathematicians of the era before specialisation have worked in number theory in some form, and part of what appeals to me is the sheer background one needs to do modern number theory. Vast swathes of the field require spending a long time understanding other fields that originally seem unrelated, such as analysis, algebra and geometry (although, in the modern context, it's so well-known that number theory is ripe for applications of other fields that I'm not sure how unexpected people find it anymore; I certainly was very surprised the first time I learnt, say, that the distribution of prime numbers depended on the zeroes of the -function).

Within number theory, I lean towards the analytic, combinatorial and elementary aspects of number theory; I know very little about the algebraic aspects of number theory beyond some basic algebraic number theory.

In particular, I am interested in additive number theory, multiplicative number theory (including the theory of the Riemann function), sieve theory and additive combinatorics. I am also interested tangentially in computational aspects of number theory, and the applications of additive combinatorics and number theory to theoretical computer science.
